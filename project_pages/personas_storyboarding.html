PART 1: PREPARATION

Accessible Interface

I chose to examine the Snackpass order kiosk at Ten One Tea House in order to understand how and why users interact with this product.

Sketch and Description of Interface

The kiosk at Ten One allows for contactless ordering of drinks and helps with reducing the wait time for ordering. It consists of a screen that allows customers to choose their drinks and a stand with a card reader. When customers first arrive, the home screen contains a "Tap to Continue" caption that informs users to tap anywhere on the screen to continue with the ordering process.

Once customers tap the screen, they are met with a new interface that displays the menu. The menu contains information about the different categories of drinks, the names of each drink, the price of the drink, images of the drink, etc.

Once the customer taps on the drink they want to order, they are presented with options about how they want their drink and other add-ons. The options are spilt into categories (size, milk, milk foam, toppings, ice level, and sugar level). If the category is required, then it ensures that users select an options. Additionally, if certain options within a category cost extra money, the screen will disply exactly how much will be added to the total.

After users have selected all their add-ons, they can either add more drinks or check out. On the checkout page, the users are able to see every item they have in their cart (along with any choices/add-ons they selected) and the total price of their order.

Finally, when customers place the order, they are first presented with a screen that allows them to select a tip. Once they have selected the tip and continued, they are presented with a screen informing them how to make the payment. From here, they can use the card reader attached to the kiok in order to pay. This card reader allows for cards (insert or tap to pay) as well as Apple Pay. After the user pays, a screen appears that asks the user for a phone number and an option to select whether they want to collect their Snackpass rewards. Once the user enters the phone number and selects and option for rewards, another screen appears. The final screen displays the user's order number. Then, when the user picks up their order, there is a recipt attached to the drink, so they know exactly what they ordered and how much it cost.

PART 2: OBSERVATIONS

Objective Observations

User 1
  • Hesitated when first arriving at kiosk - looked at kiosk, turned to walked away, but decided to proceed with kiosk
  • Tapped the home screen once, nothing happend (screen did not change), and tapped it again
  • Scrolled all the way to the bottom of the menu and then back to the top again
  • Clicked on a few different drink categories at the top of the menu page
  • Scrolled through the add-ons/options page before selecting any of the options for any of the categories
  • Warning saying they didn't select an option for a particular category appeared when adding to cart for the first time
  • Took time to look through the checkout page
  • At the tips page, selected one option before deciding on a different option
  • Tapped card for payment method
  • Entered their phone number and opted out of the Snackpass reward after some hesitation
User 2
  • A pair of friends - the first friend was ordering but the second friend payed
  • Second friend walked up to kiosk first, and tapped the home screen
  • First friend looked through options (scrolled through a little bit of the menu) but did not choose a drink
  • Second friend recommended a drink (Grapefruit Tea), and the first one chose that
  • First friend selected options in the add-ons/options page, but stopped at the toppings section
  • Second friend recommended selecting aloe, and first friend selected aloe
  • Once second friend added to cart, frist friend immediately pressed continue on the page displaying cart content and price
  • Second friend selected a tip and inserted their card to pay, entered their phone number, and opted for the Snackpass rewards
User 3
  • Walked up to kios, and tapped screen
  • Immediately selected category (Milk Tea) from the categories listed at the top of the menu page
  • Immediately selected the regular milk tea and proceeded to the add-ons/options page
  • On the add-ons/options page, went through the add-ons quickly and selected more than one topping
  • Did not take time to read the contents of the cart or the price - immediately checked out
  • Hesitated when selecting a tip - initially chose one options, but switched to another
  • Used phone/Apple Pay to complete the transaction
  • Typed in phone number and opted for Snackpass reward with no hesitation

Key Observations

The first person I actually observed was someone who did not end up using the kiosk - they walked over to the kiosk, but eventually left. This person ended up ordering at the counter and payed in cash. Since the kiosk only accepts card/Apple Pay, this person could not interact with the kiosk. The major difference between some of the users was the amount of time it took for them to order the drink itself. Certain users took the time to scroll through the menu and look the options whereas others immediately clicked on a category and a certain drink. Furthermore, the users that immediately selected a drink to order were also the users that did not take the time to review their purchase at the checkout screen and the users that opted in for the Snackpass rewards.

Interview Summary

Just as a reminder, here are the questions I asked when interviewing the users about their experience with the kiosk:

  1. Why did you choose to place your order through the kiosk?
  2. What would keep you from using this kiosk?
  3. What was easy about placing your order using the kiosk? What was difficult?
  4. Is there anything you would add, remove, or change to improve your experience with using the kios to order?
  5. Was there any particular feature that stood out to you? Why?

Here are the main points and takeaways from the interviews:

  • User 3 and the second friend for User 2 noted that were familiar with the interface and chose to to use it to order rather than order at the counter because they wanted the Snackpass rewards. User 1, who wasn't familiar with the interface decided to try it out because it looked like an simple, easy way to order a drink.
  • User 3 and the second friend for User 2 who were already familiar with the kiosk found the home screen to be useless and thought that removing it would improve the speed of ordering and, thus, their experience. However, user 1 who was not familiar with the interface actually appreciated the home screen because they would not have known what the kiosk was for/whether they should use it had the home screen not appeared.
  • A major component that user 3 wanted changed was the add-ons/options page. They noted that the Snackpass app saves previous orders which typically saves them a lot of effort when ordering. They wish that the kiosk would also save their information because they like ordering the same drink with the same add-ons each time they are here.
  • Interestingly, all of the interviewed users the ordering process to be straightforward and simple to understand. While User 1 did not find anything particularly difficult, they did find the Snackpass reward feature confusing. They noted that they had not heard about Snackpass which is why they opted out.

PART 3: PERSONAS

Persona 1

Crunched Carson is a student who is having a bad day and is in a time crunch because he is already late to his section meeting. Unlike the Snackpass app, the kiosk has you input your drink add-ons and preferences everytime you use it to order. While ordering his drink, Carson's friend calls him telling him he'll be late if he doesn't leave Ten One now. Frustrated and rushed, he does not notice that he accidentally selected the wrong topping. Additionally, he scrolls past a required selection which slows him down even more since he has to go back and select an option in this category.

Persona 2

Confused Carly is a freshman who has never been to Ten One before and doesn't notice that the kiosk does not accept cash. When she first enters the shop, she notices the kiosk and decides to order from there because she hates having to order in person. After she spends some time scrolling through the menu, she decides on the drink she wants and order. At this point, Carly realizes that the machine does not accept cash and starts to get worried. However, she realizes that the machine might accept Apple Pay since most modern machines do. Luckily, it does and she pays using her phone. Since Carly is a freshman, she has never heard of Snackpass, but now that the knows Snackpass gives rewards, she decides to download the app and tell her friends about it.
